Quick Start Guide: Creating Rules in Asana

Getting Started​

Before we begin let's go over the building blocks you will use to create a rule:

  • Triggers: Conditions that initiate a rule.
  • Actions: The automated responses that follow a trigger.
  • Conditions: Specific criteria that determine whether an action should be executed.
  • Sections: Organizational units within a project that group tasks based on stages or types. Used in actions to move tasks.
  • Due Dates: The deadlines assigned to tasks. Can be used as triggers or actions.
  • Assignees: The team members responsible for completing tasks. Can be used to trigger specific actions.
  • Priorities: Levels of importance assigned to tasks. Can also be used to trigger specific actions.
  • Custom Fields: User-defined fields that add extra information to tasks. They can be used as triggers or conditions for rules.
  • Integrations: Connections with other tools or applications. These can be used as triggers or actions.

How to Create a Rule​


 

1. Open Asana and sign in to your account.

2. Navigate to the project where you want to create a rule.

3. Click on the "Customize" button, usually found on the far right of your project, under your profile image.

4. Scroll down to Rules in the Customize tab and click on "Add Rule." A pop-up window will open.

5. Click on the "Create Custom Rule" button at the top to start creating a new rule.

6. Click "Choose a Trigger" and select a trigger from the list of options. This is the action that will initiate your rule. For example, "Task marked as complete".

7. After choosing a trigger, pick an action from the available options listed. This is the action you want to automatically occur when the trigger happens. For example, "Move task to a specific section".

8. (Optional) If your rule requires specific conditions (triggers) to be met before the action is executed, select and define these conditions.

9. Give your rule a clear and descriptive name so you can easily identify it later.

10. Save Your Rule: Review your rule to ensure it's set up as intended, then click "Save".

11. Test Your Rule: Test the rule by performing the trigger action to make sure the rule works as expected.

12. Congratulations! You've successfully created a rule for your project in Asana. This will help automate your project management process, saving time and ensuring consistency across your tasks.
